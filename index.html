    Mae Jeminson

    American engineer, physician and former astronaut. Known for being the first black woman to travel into space. She talks fluently russian, japanesse, swahili and also english. Since little, she became interested in archeology and astronomy. At the age of 16 she attended Stanford University and graduated in chemical engineering. Later on, she got a doctoral degree in medicine from the Cornell University. In 1993, she quit NASA and started the Jeminson Group.

    Hypatia

    Known as one of the best mathematicians and astronomers of antiquity. To attend her school, scholars use to travel really long distances. Hypatia was a greek speaker daughter of Theon of Alexandria who develop a famous student´s version of Euclid´s Elements. Among her work we can found a mathematical model that predicted the movement of planets(epicycles), great apportations to algebra and commentaries in Conic Sections articles. She was cruelty murdered.

    Margaret Hamilton

    American computer scientist, systems engineer and business owner. She directed the Software Engineering Division of the MIT Instrumentation Laboratory, which develop on-board flight software for NASA´s Apollo program. She founded later on, the Higher Order Software company. On 2016, she received the Presidential Medal of Freedom for her leading work to the development of on-board software for Apollo missions.

    Jane Goodall

    Ethologist and conservationist, changed the definition of being human due to her multiple investigations with wild chimpanzees in Gombe Stream National Park in Tanzania, where she make observations that challengue well-accepted ideas : (1) chimps are omnivores, not herbivores and even hunt; (2) chimps use tools; and (3) chimps make their tools (a trait previously used to define humans).

    Mary Anning

    An impoverished, self-taught fossil hunter whose remarkable discoveries apved the way for modern plaontology. Trough her discoveries, she expanded human knowledge of ancient life. Among her discoveries, it is a complete skeleton of a plesiosaurus, a four-limbed extinct marine reptile and the first pterosaur.

    Ada Lovelace

    Known as the world´s first computer programmer, long before there were computers. In 1843, Babbage was developing the Analytical Engine and asked Lovelace to help his translate his notes, she added her own that today we know, suggest the invention of a computer language.

    Marie Curie

    The first person ever to be awarded two Nobel Prizes. The first one for the discovery of radioactivity by examinning many substances and minerals and finding that de pitchblende was more radioactive than the uranium. And the second for the discovery of polinium and radium. In 1910 she produced radium as pure metal, proving the existence of a new element. Radioactive compounds are important in medicine and in multiple scientific areas.
